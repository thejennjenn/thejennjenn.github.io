Deal Dash
Gamifying the Shopping Experience

Need

How might get shoppers to come back again and again?

Goal

Leverage a shopper’s sense of urgency and excitement by gamifying the shopping experience to increase retention and sales.

Results

Increase in engagement, retention, and sales. Shoppers who engaged with and purchased products in Deal Dash were more likely to return to buy more products and therefore increased sales.

Deal Dash is a feature within Wish that gamifies the shopping experience by allowing shoppers to play and redeem rewards.

Approach

Games can be addicting and fun, what if shopping were the same? While researching the psychology of gaming, I discovered several patterns:

Colors & animations = delight
Timed events = excitement & urgency
Reward = positive conditioning

What if we adapted this concept of gaming to shopping?
What would that look like?
How would it function?
How do we effectively communicate this to shoppers?
Will they understand its value?

Ideate

Concept: Once a day, shoppers can visit Deal Dash and go on a timed shopping spree for extra discounted products for a limited time of 10 minutes. Shoppers can come back every 24 hours to play Deal Dash again.

Uh oh...

After getting feedback, we realized that shoppers don’t understand Deal Dash or the value being offered. Problems and assumptions include:

Shoppers are not reading the text.
Context clues are not clear enough to convey the concept of a game.
Shoppers don't know what is happening.
No one was coming back to play Deal Dash.

Back to the drawing board

Left: Walkthrough continue. Middle: Shopping spree page with extra discounted products for only 10 minutes. Right: Example of a Product Details page of a selected product.

What we did differently...

In the previous iteration the value was lost. Context clues such as the UI and text failed to convey the concept of Deal Dash nor was it fun or exciting. In this new version, our goal was to make the “game” concept and the value of getting extra discounted products more explicit, as well as engaging and fun.

To convey the game concept, we added more context clues such as color, animations, and graphics. We gave shoppers a sense of control of the outcome of the Deal Dash by allowing them to spin the wheel and unlocked x number of extra discounted products for their Deal Dash. We incorporated a 10-minute countdown timer to create a sense of urgency and excitement as shoppers race against the clock to explore and add products to cart before time runs out.

To convey the value, we used phrases that were more clear and explicit like “Once a day sale on extra discounted products,” and “Shop the best deals of the day before time runs out,” as opposed to the previous, “The wait is over. Even more discounts on selected items. Shopping spree lasts 10 minutes.” We also included a walkthrough tutorial and pop-up tutorial to further convey the value.

By making deal dash available once a day and rewarding shoppers with extra discounted products, this conditions and encourages more shoppers to return to Wish to interact with Deal Dash. Additionally, the sense of scarcity in making it available once a day helps to create desire and longing.

Results

The outcome included a boost in engagement and retention. More shoppers were interacting and coming back to take part in Deal Dash. Those who purchased products in Deal Dash were more likely to return to Wish to purchase products again, contributing to an increase in sales.
