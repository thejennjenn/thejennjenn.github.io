Deal Dash
Gamifying the Shopping Experience

Need

How might we increase purchases?

Goal

Leverage a shopper’s sense of urgency and excitement by gamifying the shopping experience in order to increase sales.

Results

Increase in engagement, retention, and sales. Shoppers who engaged with and purchased products in Deal Dash were more likely to return to buy more products and therefore increased sales.

Wish is an ecommerce app that offers relevant and interesting products for cost conscious bargain shoppers. Deal Dash, a feature within Wish, gamifies the shopping experience by allowing shoppers to play and redeem rewards and discounts.

Approach

Games can be addicting and fun, what if shopping were the same? While researching the psychology of gaming, I discovered several patterns:

Colors & animations = delight
Timed events = excitement & urgency
Reward = positive conditioning

What if we adapted this concept of gaming to shopping?
What would that look like?
How would it function?
How do we effectively communicate this to shoppers?
Will they understand its value?

Ideate

Concept: Once a day, shoppers can visit Deal Dash and go on a timed shopping spree for extra discounted products for a limited time of 10 minutes. Shoppers can come back every 24 hours to play Deal Dash again.

Uh oh...

Shoppers don’t understand Deal Dash or the value being offered. Assumptions include:

Shoppers are not reading the text.
Context clues are not clear enough to convey the concept of a game.
Shoppers don't know what is happening.

Back to the drawing board

What we did differently...

In the previous concept the value was lost, the UI and text failed to explain the concept of Deal Dash no was it fun or exciting. In this new version, our goal was to make the “gaming” concept and the value of getting extra discounted products more explicit.

To convey the game concept, we added more context clues such as color, animations, and graphics. We gave shoppers a sense of control of the outcome of the Deal Dash by allowing them to spin the wheel and unlocked x number of extra discounted products for their Deal Dash. We incorporated at 10 minute countdown to create a sense of urgency and excitement as shoppers race against time to explore and add products to cart before time runs out.

To convey the value, we used phrases that were more clear and explicit like “Once a day sale on extra discounted products,” and “Shop the best deals of the day before time runs out,” as opposed to the previous, “The wait is over. Even more discounts on selected items. Shopping spree lasts 10 minutes.” We also included a walkthrough tutorial and pop-up tutorial to further conveys the value.

Results

The outcome included a boost in engagement and retention. More shoppers were interacting and coming back every so often to take part in Deal Dash. Those who purchased products in Deal Dash were more likely to return to Wish to purchase products again, contributing to an increase in sales.
